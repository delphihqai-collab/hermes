# AM Churn Intervention Brief

Used by: AM → HERMES (immediately on At Risk or Critical trigger)
Rule: Filed before any client contact. AM never communicates a problem to a client without HERMES approval on tone and content first.

```
CHURN INTERVENTION BRIEF — [Client Name]
─────────────────────────────────────────
Client:              [Name, company, sector]
Health tier:         [At Risk / Critical]
Date filed:          [DD/MM/YYYY]
AM:                  [Name]

── WHAT TRIGGERED THIS ─────────────────
Trigger:             [Specific signal — usage drop / complaint / invoice / competitor / unresponsive]
Date first observed: [DD/MM/YYYY]
Evidence:            [Data, message, or call notes that confirm the signal]
How long since last healthy signal: [N days]

── CLIENT CONTEXT ──────────────────────
Last meaningful interaction: [Date, format, what was said]
Current usage:       [Core feature activity — specific numbers]
Payment status:      [Paid / Overdue — N days]
Any open complaints: [Y/N — describe]
Any recent changes at their end: [Staff change / new location / regulatory event / etc.]

── AM ASSESSMENT ───────────────────────
Root cause hypothesis: [What the AM believes is actually happening — be specific, not vague]
Risk level:          [At Risk — likely recoverable / Critical — contract at risk]
What the client needs right now: [Practical action, reassurance, product fix, intervention call, etc.]

── PROPOSED INTERVENTION ───────────────
Recommended action:  [Specific — intervention call / product fix / concession discussion / etc.]
Proposed message tone: [Warm and proactive / Direct and solutions-focused / Escalation acknowledgement]
Draft opening:       [One or two sentences — how AM would open the conversation if approved]
What NOT to say:     [Any topic or tone to avoid given this client's profile]

Waiting for HERMES approval before any client contact.
```
