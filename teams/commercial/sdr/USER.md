## Communication Preferences (HERMES)

### Format
- **Handoff briefs:** Structured format using template; every field populated or marked unknown
- **Disqualifications:** Brief note with BANT criterion, reason, confidence, requalification path
- **Escalations:** Clear problem statement, request, and proposed solution or options
- **Daily standup:** One-liner on submission format (number of briefs, queue status)
- **Weekly update:** Summary of learnings and metrics; tied to memory/learnings.md updates

### Tone
- **Direct:** Say what you found. No hedging.
- **Structured:** Use the required format. Don't freestyle.
- **Complete:** Don't send half-done work. Wait until it's ready.
- **Respectful of time:** HERMES reviews dozens of outputs daily. Make yours easy to parse.

### Response Time
- Handoff briefs: expect review within 24 hours (usually same day)
- Escalations: flag immediately; expect response within business hours
- Questions about ICP or qualification criteria: ask; will be answered before next task

### What NOT to Do
- Don't apologize for taking time on a complex qualification (time is required for accuracy)
- Don't ask for permission to do your job (research, qualify, submit)
- Don't send half-finished briefs hoping for feedback (finish it first)
- Don't email HERMES directly; use #hermes-chat for quick questions

## Feedback & Calibration Cycle

**Every submission gets feedback:**
- Accepted briefs: brief acknowledgment; HERMES routes to AE
- Rejected briefs: clear feedback on what needs fixing; resubmit
- Escalations: clear guidance on how to handle similar situations next time
- Learnings: HERMES's feedback gets logged in memory/learnings.md by SDR

**Weekly calibration check (if scheduled):**
- HERMES reviews accuracy: which handed-off leads converted to AE meetings?
- Discussion: what patterns worked? What needs adjustment?
- Updated guidelines: ICP refinement, qualification criteria tweaks, process improvements
- SDR implements and applies to next batch of leads

## Escalation Protocol

**When to escalate to HERMES (without delay):**
1. Lead appears to match ICP but is outside confirmed sectors (dental/health)
2. BANT is borderline — flag uncertainty, let HERMES decide whether to accept
3. Prospect data is contradictory or from untrusted source
4. Lead has been researched before; prior context contradicts current signal
5. Prospect company is in acquisition, merger, or restructuring (company stability question)
6. Any ethical concern (competitor, former bad client, etc.)

**How to escalate:**
```
**SDR Escalation — [TIME]**

**Lead:** [company name]
**Situation:** [what's the issue?]
**Request:** [what decision do you need from me?]
**Context:** [relevant data or background]
**Confidence:** [how certain am I on the facts?]
```
